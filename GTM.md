# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Strategy

> Go-to-market plan for SpendLens: how to acquire the first 100 users,
> position the product, and identify the right early adopters.

---

## One-Line Positioning

**SpendLens tells you exactly which AI subscriptions your startup is wasting money on — in 10 minutes.**

---

## Target Customer

### Primary: Seed-stage startup founders (2–15 person teams)
- Feel the pain of rising AI tool costs but have no system to track them
- Make purchasing decisions themselves (no procurement department)
- Respond to concrete numbers ("you're wasting $X/month") over abstract advice
- Hang out on Twitter/X, Hacker News, and Indie Hackers

### Secondary: Operations leads at Series A startups (15–50 people)
- Starting to feel pressure to justify SaaS spend to investors
- Have enough tools that overlap is genuinely occurring
- May have a finance person asking questions about burn

---

## Problem We're Solving

AI tool subscriptions are the new SaaS sprawl. A 10-person startup can easily
accumulate $500–800/month in AI subscriptions with no clear owner, no utilisation
tracking, and significant overlap (e.g. Cursor + GitHub Copilot doing the same job).

Unlike traditional SaaS, AI tools are adopted bottom-up by individual contributors,
making it hard for founders to even know what's active.

---

## Channels

### 1. Hacker News — Show HN post
- Title: "Show HN: I built a 10-minute AI spend audit for startups"
- Target: front page or top of Show HN
- Why it fits: HN skews technical founders who feel this pain directly
- Expected: 200–500 visitors on launch day if it hits

### 2. Twitter/X
- Post the "$0 → your AI waste number in 10 minutes" angle
- Tag relevant accounts: @levelsio, @patio11, indie hacker community
- Share the savings number as a hook ("our 8-person team was wasting $340/month")

### 3. Indie Hackers
- Post in "Show IH" with the product story and early metrics
- Community of bootstrappers who care deeply about cost efficiency

### 4. YC Startup School Slack / Alumni Networks
- Direct message to founders in relevant stage channels
- "Would you try a free audit of your AI spend?" is a low-friction ask

### 5. Cold outreach to startup ops communities
- Notion's startup community, Lenny's Newsletter Slack
- Frame as a tool, not a pitch

## Pricing Strategy (Post-MVP)

| Tier | Price | What You Get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | One audit, basic savings estimate |
| Pro | $29/month | Unlimited audits, team sharing, history, email report |
| Team | $99/month | Multi-user, Slack alerts, monthly re-audit reminders |

Freemium is intentional: the audit result itself is the hook.
If the tool finds $300/month in waste, paying $29 for the full report is obvious ROI.

## Competitive Positioning

| Tool | What They Do | SpendLens Difference |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| Cledara | SaaS spend tracking (requires integrations) | No integration needed, 10-min audit |
| Zylo | Enterprise SaaS management | Built for startups, not Fortune 500 |
| Manual spreadsheet | What most founders actually do | Faster, AI-powered, actionable output |

SpendLens wins on speed-to-insight: no integrations, no setup, just a form.
